OSCP Challenge

I will not go into background and waste time and would rather come straight to the point. OSCP preparation takes hard work and consistent efforts. The OSCP exam challenge involves exploiting five main machines. Each machine will have different marking depending upon the difficulty level of compromising it. The main aspects of the exam are –

  • 24-hour exam with available lab machines carrying different marks.
  • A PDF report of the exam as in how did you compromise the host. What were the steps taken? Vulnerabilities exploited. etc.
  • A PDF report of the lab machines, which you exploited while preparing for the OSCP challenge. This will award you bonus 10 marks.
  • A try harder approach.

Preliminary preparation – Ground Zero

OSCP course itself is vast and wholesome for a person to impart skills required for a penetration tester or ethical hacker. The concepts involve lot of base build up when it comes to different concepts such as assembly language, python and Linux.

Before starting with the actual course I suggest you should start 2 months in advance and get yourself familiar with following available resources to get hands-on assembly language, python and Linux: –

I cannot stress enough for having a basic background before appearing for a wholesome exam like OSCP. In my view, a person should be familiar with following areas before attempting the exam: –

  • Basic knowledge of Linux commands and OS – According to me this is the basic step in starting your OSCP labs. A person should have basic knowledge and understanding of Linux command line especially basic OS level commands.
  • Knowledge on Linux and assembly language   – This is an essential step in OSCP because it helps with problem like buffer overflow during the OSCP challenge. For this, I would recommend to go back to the basics and watch all related video tutorials. The ones, which I referred to, were – https://youtu.be/K0g-twyhmQ4 (Assembly Primer for Hackers (Part 1) System Organization), https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RF7DF4kfs1E (Buffer Overflow Primer Part 1 (Smashing the Stack)), and windows assembly language mega primer.
  • Security background – It is essential that one should have a good information security background. I would recommend if you are, a fresher do not approach OSCP course. At least take 3-4 years of beginner level experience before taking up the lab course. A person should have basic networking knowledge, and of commonly used tools such as Nmap, Tcpdump, Wireshark etc.
  • Practice some hacking – I did not do this part because I realized this after I took OSCP lab course. You can go and practice some hacking free of cost from commonly available websites such as – https://www.vulnhub.com/, https://www.hackthis.co.uk/, https://www.hackthissite.org/ etc.

Course guidelines

OSCP course mainly comprises of 300 page of PDF and video tutorial from Offensive Security. According to me, these are more than enough to build fundamental knowledge for pen testing with Kali. They give you enough details of using tools such as NMAP, Netcat, Sqlmap etc., which are essential tools for any kind of enumeration and exploitation.

Apart from this, I would recommend following links and cheat sheet, which came handy while I was practicing in the lab environment using different tools. You can refer to these links and I would suggest place it in your notes. I would also be discussing note-taking strategy in my next section.

Note making strategy

Here I have used simple strategy from Mike Czumak’s note taking strategy at https://www.securitysift.com/offsec-pwb-oscp/

Please follow the above link for Keepnote with dropbox approach which seems most effective me.

 

Exam approach

This is the final and most important part of OSCP exam taking. First, compile all the lab network machines, which you compromised as a part of practice for OSCP exam through your keep note. Secondly, take screenshots for all lab exercises and steps you used while exploiting the machines.

Practice buffer overflow as much as possible using PDF guide and videos. Practice things like checking the return address of the ESP etc. This is a very important part as it involves a buffer overflow machine in the exam.

Next step practice some python scripting. If possible, come with some script to quickly enumerate your targeted network with help of those. Some scripts are also available online but use at your own discretion. I personally did not use any script in the exams and used the basic available tools such as Nmap, dirb, nikto etc.

Finally, before the day of the exam take enough rest. I cannot stress enough the importance of taking rest, as you will be up for a 48-hour long exam. Eat properly and take enough sleep.  On the exam day start with easier machines first. Keep and an NMAP scan started for all the machines. Identify open ports. Start with the machine, which has most easily exploitable vulnerability of ports. Then slowly move towards difficult ones. Keep buffer overflow for later as it needs some patience and time. Perform privilege escalation, as it is the most time consuming task. Keep snacks and drinks handy like some confectionary items such as Tim tams, chocolates, candies and coke. As you will certainly need lot of sugar in the process as lot of energy will be consumed.

Conclusion:

With the above, I would like to end on the note by saying that using proper preparation time, continuous practice and never say die approach you can certainly be an OSCP!
